## 🎨 DESIGN TIPS (Optional but Helpful)

**Color Scheme**:
- Primary: Dark blue (professional, trustworthy)
- Accent: Green (positive, growth)
- Background: White or light gray (clean, modern)

**Typography**:
- Headings: Sans-serif, bold (e.g., Helvetica, Arial)
- Body: Clear, readable font (12-14pt minimum)
- Do NOT make text small to fit more

**Images**:
- Use professional audit/business photos if available
- AI-related visuals (neural networks, data patterns) optional
- Screenshots of your platform (Slide 8 most important)

**Avoid**:
- Too many bullets (max 3-4 per slide)
- Small text that requires squinting
- Flashy animations (keeps focus on message)
- Jargon without explanation

## 📝 SPEAKER NOTES (What to Remember)

**Opening (Slide 1-2)**:
- Confident greeting
- Problem statement clear
- Pause for emphasis on pain points

**Middle (Slide 3-7)**:
- Slow down for numbers
- Make eye contact with different audience members
- Pause after big statements ("87% accuracy")

**Demo (Slide 8)**:
- Speak naturally
- Describe what you're clicking
- Pause for audience reactions

**Close (Slide 9-10)**:
- Confidence on traction
- Clear call-to-action
- Open for questions

## 🎯 WHAT SUCCESS LOOKS LIKE

**You'll Know You Nailed It If**:

After presentation, someone asks one of these:
- "When can we start a pilot?" (Clients)
- "What's your funding ask?" (Investors)
- "How do we integrate this?" (Technical team)
- "Can you handle our jurisdiction?" (Regulators)

Any of these = SUCCESS. You've moved from "interested" to "specific next step."
