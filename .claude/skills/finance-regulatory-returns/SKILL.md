# Regulatory Returns and Filings -- Full Process Guide

This skill covers the preparation and filing of regulatory returns across UK regulators (FCA, PRA, Bank of England, Companies House, HMRC) for financial services firms and all UK companies.

---

## 1. FCA Investment Firms -- MIFIDPRU

### 1.1 Capital Requirements (K-Factors)
- MIFIDPRU applies to FCA-authorised investment firms (replacing IFPRU/BIPRU from January 2022)
- K-factors determine own funds requirements based on risk to clients, risk to markets, and risk to firm:
  - Risk to client (RtC): K-AUM (assets under management), K-CMH (client money held), K-ASA (assets safeguarded and administered), K-COH (client orders handled)
  - Risk to market (RtM): K-NPR (net position risk), K-CMG (clearing margin given)
  - Risk to firm (RtF): K-TCD (trading counterparty default), K-DTF (daily trading flow), K-CON (concentration risk)

### 1.2 Own Funds
- CET1: share capital, share premium, retained earnings, other reserves (less deductions)
- AT1: qualifying perpetual instruments
- Tier 2: qualifying subordinated debt
- Own funds requirement: higher of permanent minimum capital, fixed overhead requirement (FOR = 25% of fixed overheads), and K-factor requirement

### 1.3 ICARA Process
- Internal Capital Adequacy and Risk Assessment
- Covers: business model assessment, risk identification, wind-down planning, stress testing, harm scenarios (clients, market, firm)
- Annual review and board approval

## 2. FCA Regulatory Returns

### 2.1 FIN Returns
- FIN070: balance sheet (quarterly or semi-annually depending on firm category)
- FIN071: income statement
- FIN072: capital adequacy (own funds and K-factor requirements)

### 2.2 MIF Returns
- MIF001-MIF007: supplementary MIFIDPRU reporting templates
- Cover K-factor calculations, concentration risk, liquid assets, group capital
- Frequency: quarterly for SNI firms, monthly for larger firms

### 2.3 Filing
- Filed via FCA RegData platform
- Deadlines: 30 business days after quarter-end (non-SNI); 60 business days (SNI)

---

## 3. PRA Banks -- CRD VI / CRR III

### 3.1 COREP (Common Reporting)
- Own funds composition and deductions (C01-C05)
- Capital requirements: credit risk (standardised/IRB), market risk, operational risk, CVA risk (C07-C09)
- Large exposures (C27-C29)
- Leverage ratio (C40-C43)
- Frequency: quarterly

### 3.2 FINREP (Financial Reporting)
- Balance sheet, income statement, loan quality, forbearance, non-performing exposures
- Required for IFRS-reporting credit institutions
- Frequency: quarterly (some templates semi-annually or annually)

### 3.3 Liquidity Reporting
- LCR (Liquidity Coverage Ratio): HQLA / net cash outflows over 30-day stress >= 100%
- NSFR (Net Stable Funding Ratio): available stable funding / required stable funding >= 100%
- Filed via Bank of England BEEDS platform
- Frequency: monthly (LCR), quarterly (NSFR)

## 4. PRA Insurance -- Solvency II QRTs

### 4.1 Key Quantitative Reporting Templates
- S.02.01: balance sheet (Solvency II valuation basis)
- S.05.01: premiums, claims, and expenses by line of business
- S.12.01: life technical provisions
- S.17.01: non-life technical provisions
- S.23.01: own funds (composition and tiering)
- S.25.01: Solvency Capital Requirement (SCR) calculation

### 4.2 MCR Calculation
- Minimum Capital Requirement: floor below which the firm cannot operate
- Linear function of technical provisions and written premiums
- MCR must be between 25% and 45% of the SCR

### 4.3 SFCR Publication
- Solvency and Financial Condition Report: annual public disclosure
- Covers: business and performance, governance, risk profile, valuation for solvency, capital management
- Published on firm's website

## 5. ICAAP/ILAAP (Banks)

### 5.1 ICAAP
- Pillar 2A capital for risks not fully captured by Pillar 1 (credit concentration, IRRBB, operational risk, pension risk)
- Stress testing: firm-specific and PRA-prescribed scenarios
- Document structure: executive summary, business model, governance, risk appetite, capital planning, stress results, buffers

### 5.2 ILAAP
- Assessment of adequate liquidity resources
- Liquidity stress testing: idiosyncratic, market-wide, and combined scenarios
- Survival period analysis and funding diversification
- Annual board approval; submitted to PRA on request

---

## 6. ICARA (Investment Firms)

### 6.1 Structure
- Assessment of adequacy of own funds and liquid assets
- Wind-down planning: orderly wind-down over realistic period, estimating costs and timing
- Harm scenarios: potential harms to clients, market integrity, and the firm

### 6.2 Liquid Assets Requirement
- Maintain liquid assets covering at least one third of fixed overhead requirement
- Plus additional needs from wind-down and harm analysis
- Qualifying: cash, CET1 instruments, government bonds meeting LCR HQLA criteria

## 7. CASS Returns

### 7.1 CMAR (Client Money and Asset Return)
- Monthly submission to FCA (within 15 business days of month-end)
- Reports: client money held, client money requirement, client assets held, reconciliation status
- Filed via FCA RegData

### 7.2 Client Money Calculation
- Alternative approach: all money received into client accounts plus accrued interest, less amounts properly paid out
- Normal approach: sum of individual client entitlements
- Daily internal reconciliation; external reconciliation at least every 25 business days

### 7.3 CASS Resolution Pack
- Annual submission to FCA
- Contains: client money and asset positions, trust status, third-party details
- Facilitates rapid return of client assets on firm failure

### 7.4 CASS Audit Report
- Annual reasonable assurance report by firm's auditors
- Covers CASS 5 (client money, insurance), CASS 6 (custody), CASS 7 (client money, investment firms)
- Filed with FCA annually

## 8. Bank of England Returns

### 8.1 Statistical Returns
- Form BT: balance sheet (monthly and quarterly)
- Form PL: profit and loss (quarterly)
- Form BE: banking and economic data
- Filed via BEEDS

### 8.2 Stress Testing Submissions
- PRA concurrent stress test (annual for major UK banks)
- Bank of England Annual Cyclical Scenario (ACS)
- Submissions: stressed P&L, capital trajectories, impairment projections, market risk impacts

## 9. Companies House Filings

### 9.1 Annual Accounts
- Private companies: 9 months after year-end; public companies: 6 months
- Small company: abbreviated or filleted accounts
- Micro-entity: very simplified micro-entity accounts
- Medium/large: full accounts (may file filleted for Companies House)

### 9.2 Confirmation Statement (CS01)
- Annual filing confirming company details (officers, PSC, SIC codes, share capital)
- Due at least every 12 months from incorporation anniversary or last CS01

### 9.3 Other Filings
- Change of directors: AP01 (appointment), TM01 (resignation)
- Change of PSC: PSC01-PSC09
- Change of registered office: AD01

### 9.4 Late Filing Penalties
- Private: GBP 150 (up to 1 month late) to GBP 1,500 (over 6 months)
- Public: GBP 750 (up to 1 month) to GBP 7,500 (over 6 months)
- Penalties double for consecutive late filings

## 10. HMRC Returns

### 10.1 CT600 and Computations
- Filing: 12 months after AP end; payment: 9 months and 1 day (or QIPs)
- iXBRL format with accounts and computations

### 10.2 VAT
- Quarterly or monthly via MTD; deadline: 1 month and 7 days after period-end
- FS firms: typically partially exempt

### 10.3 PAYE/NI (RTI)
- Real Time Information: FPS and EPS on or before each pay date
- Annual P11D for benefits in kind: 6 July following tax year

### 10.4 CIS Returns
- Construction Industry Scheme: monthly returns for contractors
- Verify subcontractors before payments

---

## 11. Filing Timetable

### 11.1 Monthly
- CMAR: 15 business days after month-end
- PAYE RTI: on or before pay date
- VAT (monthly reporters): 1 month and 7 days
- PRA/BoE statistical returns: per BEEDS schedule

### 11.2 Quarterly
- FCA FIN/MIF: 30 BD (non-SNI) / 60 BD (SNI) after quarter-end
- PRA COREP/FINREP: 30-45 business days per PRA schedule
- VAT (quarterly): 1 month and 7 days
- QIPs: months 7, 10, 13, 16 (large) or 3, 6, 9, 12 (very large)

### 11.3 Annual
- Companies House accounts: 9 months (private) / 6 months (public) after year-end
- CT600: 12 months after AP end
- SFCR: 14 weeks after year-end
- CASS resolution pack: per FCA schedule
- P11D: 6 July following tax year

---

## 12. Penalties

### 12.1 FCA
- Late submission: fixed penalty plus daily rate by firm size and return type
- Escalating enforcement for persistent non-compliance

### 12.2 PRA
- Section 206 FSMA penalties for late or inaccurate returns
- Supervisory measures for material non-compliance

### 12.3 HMRC
- CT600 late filing: GBP 100 (3 months), GBP 200 (after 3 months), 10% of tax (6 months), further 10% (12 months)
- Late payment interest on unpaid tax
- VAT: penalty points regime (from January 2023)

### 12.4 Companies House
- Automatic late filing penalties (see section 9.4)
- Criminal offence for directors failing to file

## 13. Financial Services Specific

### 13.1 Pillar 3 Disclosure
- Annual public disclosure (CRR Art 431-455) for banks and investment firms
- Covers: risk management, capital adequacy, credit/market/operational risk, remuneration
- Large banks: semi-annual for key metrics, quarterly for selected disclosures

### 13.2 Remuneration Disclosure (CRD)
- Annual disclosure of remuneration policies and aggregate data
- Material risk takers: individual disclosure
- Deferral, malus, and clawback policies

### 13.3 PRA Liquidity Returns
- PRA110: daily liquid assets (enhanced reporting firms)
- PRA111: additional liquidity monitoring metrics
- Intraday liquidity monitoring for systemically important firms

### 13.4 Stress Testing
- PRA concurrent stress test: annual for largest UK banks (typically 8 firms)
- Bank of England ACS: exploratory scenarios
- Granular submissions: credit losses, market risk, income projections, capital impact
