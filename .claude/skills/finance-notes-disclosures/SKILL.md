# Notes to Financial Statements

Complete guide to preparing notes under both FRS 102 and IFRS, incorporating CA 2006 mandatory disclosures, drafting workflow, cross-referencing disciplines, and FS-sector overlays.

---

## 1. Note Ordering Convention

The standard ordering used in UK practice and recommended by IAS 1.114:

1. **Basis of preparation** -- legal framework, reporting standard, functional/presentation currency, going concern
2. **Accounting policies** -- material policies only (not every standard), measurement bases, specific policies driving reported amounts
3. **Critical judgements and key estimation uncertainty** -- judgements with significant effect on amounts; assumptions with risk of material adjustment within 12 months
4. **Primary statement notes: income statement** -- revenue, cost of sales, operating expenses, finance income/costs, tax, dividends, EPS
5. **Primary statement notes: balance sheet** -- intangible assets, PPE, investments, inventories, trade and other receivables, cash, trade and other payables, borrowings, provisions, deferred tax, share capital, reserves
6. **Other disclosures** -- related parties, events after the reporting period, commitments, contingencies, guarantees, ultimate controlling party

## 2. FRS 102 Mandatory Notes by Section

### S8 -- Notes to the Financial Statements (General)
- Measurement basis and other accounting policies used that are relevant to understanding
- Supporting information for items presented in the primary statements, in the order of line items and statements

### S10 -- Accounting Policies, Estimates and Errors
- Changes in accounting policy: nature, reason, amount of adjustment for current and prior periods
- Changes in estimate: nature and amount of effect in current period (and future if estimable)
- Prior period errors: nature, amount of correction for each prior period presented

### S11/S12 -- Financial Instruments
- S11 (basic): carrying amounts by category, terms and conditions of significant instruments, defaults/breaches
- S12 (other/complex): fair value disclosures, hedge accounting detail, income/expense/gains/losses by category

### S17 -- PPE
- Gross carrying amount and accumulated depreciation at start and end of period, reconciliation of movements
- Depreciation methods and useful lives; existence of title restrictions; contractual commitments for acquisition
- If revalued: date of revaluation, whether independent valuer used, carrying amount under cost model

### S20 -- Leases
- Lessee (finance leases under old FRS 102): NBV of leased assets by class, future minimum lease payments (<1yr, 2-5yr, >5yr), contingent rents
- Lessee (operating leases): future commitments (<1yr, 2-5yr, >5yr), total lease expense in period
- Note: FRS 102 was amended to align with IFRS 16 for periods beginning on or after 1 January 2026; for those periods, disclose ROU assets, lease liabilities, depreciation, interest, short-term/low-value expenses

### S23 -- Revenue
- Accounting policy for each category of revenue; amount of each category recognised in the period
- If percentage-of-completion: method used to determine stage, aggregate contract costs/profits to date

### S28 -- Employee Benefits
- Defined contribution: expense recognised
- Defined benefit: reconciliation of opening to closing plan assets and obligation, components of cost (service, interest, remeasurements), actuarial assumptions (discount rate, salary growth, mortality), sensitivity analysis

### S29 -- Income Tax
- Tax expense: current tax, deferred tax, adjustments for prior periods
- Tax reconciliation: statutory rate to effective rate
- Deferred tax: breakdown of temporary differences, unrecognised DTAs, movements in period

### S32 -- Events After the End of the Reporting Period
- Nature and estimated financial effect of material non-adjusting events
- Date accounts were authorised for issue and who authorised them

### S33 -- Related Party Disclosures
- Related party transactions: nature of relationship, amount, outstanding balances, terms (secured/unsecured, guarantees), bad debt provisions
- Key management personnel compensation (aggregate)
- Small company exemption: only transactions with directors and connected persons required (CA 2006 s413)

## 3. CA 2006 Schedule 1 Mandatory Disclosures

| Disclosure | Requirement | Reference |
|---|---|---|
| Directors' remuneration | Aggregate: emoluments, company contributions to pension schemes, compensation for loss of office | CA 2006 s412, SI 2008/410 Sch 5 |
| Highest-paid director | Emoluments and pension entitlement (if total directors' emoluments exceed GBP 200,000) | SI 2008/410 Sch 5 para 4 |
| Auditor's remuneration | Fees for audit and non-audit services (listed/large only; small exempt) | CA 2006 s494, SI 2008/489 |
| Employee costs | Wages/salaries, social security costs, pension costs; average monthly number by category | CA 2006 s411, Sch 1 para 56 |
| Share capital | Authorised (if applicable), allotted, called up, paid up; movements in period; rights of each class | CA 2006 s412, Sch 1 |
| Reserves | Movements in each reserve; transfers between reserves; non-distributable elements | CA 2006 Sch 1 |
| Guarantees and commitments | Financial commitments not provided for, guarantees, contingent liabilities | CA 2006 Sch 1 para 63-65 |
| Charges on assets | Details of charges or pledges over company assets | CA 2006 Sch 1 |

## 4. IFRS Additional Disclosure Requirements

### IFRS 7 -- Financial Instruments Disclosures
- Significance of financial instruments: carrying amounts by category (FVTPL, FVOCI, amortised cost), reclassifications
- Nature and extent of risks: credit risk (ECL analysis, staging, collateral), liquidity risk (maturity analysis of undiscounted cash flows), market risk (sensitivity analysis -- interest rate, FX, equity price)

### IFRS 8 -- Operating Segments
- Revenue, profit/loss measure, total assets/liabilities, specified items per segment
- Reconciliation of segment totals to consolidated totals
- Entity-wide disclosures: by product/service, by geography, by major customer

### IAS 19 -- Employee Benefits (Defined Benefit)
- Full reconciliation: plan assets, DBO, funded status; components of cost split between service cost (P&L), net interest (P&L), and remeasurements (OCI)
- Actuarial assumptions: discount rate, inflation, salary growth, mortality table; sensitivity per assumption

### IAS 33 -- Earnings Per Share
- Basic and diluted EPS on the face of the income statement; reconciliation of numerator and denominator
- Description of potential ordinary shares not included (anti-dilutive) and events after reporting period affecting share count

### IFRS 2 -- Share-Based Payment
- Description of arrangements; number and weighted average exercise price of options; fair value methodology (Black-Scholes, binomial); expense recognised in period; outstanding obligations

### IFRS 16 -- Leases
- ROU assets by class (with depreciation, additions, impairment); lease liabilities maturity analysis; interest expense; short-term and low-value lease expenses; variable lease payments; sale-and-leaseback gains/losses
- Lessor: finance lease receivable maturity; operating lease future receipts; unguaranteed residual values

## 5. Disclosure Checklists

- Use a disclosure checklist (published by Big 4 or ICAEW/ICAS) to ensure completeness
- Checklists map every disclosure requirement to the applicable standard and Companies Act section
- **Process**: preparer completes checklist line by line, marking each disclosure as included, not applicable (with justification), or not material (with quantification); reviewer independently verifies
- **Common omissions flagged by FRC thematic reviews**: inadequate accounting policy disclosures (boilerplate text rather than entity-specific), missing sensitivity analysis for key estimates, insufficient going concern disclosure, lack of disaggregated revenue information, incomplete related party disclosures

---

## 6. Drafting Workflow

1. **Data gathering**: collect all note inputs from subsidiary trial balances, schedules, reconciliations, legal, tax, and company secretary
2. **First draft**: populate notes template from locked TB and schedules; use prior year as starting structure
3. **Cross-reference to primary statements**: every note must tie back to a line in the primary statements; every line in the primary statements must reference a note
4. **Internal review**: preparer self-review, then independent reviewer (FC or financial reporting manager)
5. **External audit**: provide draft notes to auditors; incorporate audit adjustments; agree disclosure wording on contentious items (provisions, contingencies, going concern)
6. **Final version**: incorporate all comments; final proofread; check arithmetic (footing and cross-referencing); director approval

---

## 7. Cross-Referencing Discipline

- **Primary statements to notes**: every material line item on the balance sheet, income statement, SOCIE, and cash flow must carry a note reference number
- **Notes to primary statements**: each note opening should state the line item and primary statement it supports
- **Note-to-note**: related notes should cross-reference each other (e.g., revenue note references segment note; tax note references deferred tax on the balance sheet; provisions note references contingent liabilities note)
- **Arithmetic consistency**: totals in notes must exactly match the primary statement line; sub-totals within notes must foot correctly

---

## 8. Common Errors and FRC Findings

Based on FRC Annual Review of Corporate Reporting and thematic reviews:
- **Boilerplate accounting policies**: restating the standard rather than explaining the entity's specific policy choices
- **Missing sensitivity analysis**: key assumptions disclosed but no quantified sensitivity (IAS 1.125-129)
- **Inadequate going concern disclosure**: no detail on assumptions, available facilities, or covenant headroom
- **Revenue disaggregation**: insufficient breakdown by product, geography, or timing of transfer
- **Impairment testing**: insufficient disclosure of key assumptions, growth rates, and discount rates used in VIU calculations
- **Related party**: omitting transactions with key management personnel beyond compensation
- **IFRS 16 transition**: incomplete disclosure of practical expedients used and transition approach
- **Deferred tax**: no explanation of unrecognised DTAs and evidence supporting recognised DTAs

## 9. FS-Specific Notes

**IFRS 7 Risk Disclosures (Banking/FS)**: extensive credit risk disclosures including ECL by stage (Stage 1-3), significant increase in credit risk criteria, collateral values, sensitivity of ECL to macroeconomic scenarios; liquidity coverage ratio and net stable funding ratio cross-referenced; interest rate risk in the banking book sensitivity.

**IFRS 17 Insurance Contract Notes**: insurance revenue by portfolio; insurance service result; insurance finance income/expense; CSM rollforward; claims development tables; risk adjustment methodology and confidence level; sensitivity of technical provisions to key assumptions.

**Regulatory Capital Notes**: CET1 capital reconciliation to equity per balance sheet; RWA by risk type (credit, market, operational); leverage ratio; for investment firms (MIFIDPRU): K-factor requirements. These are typically in a separate Pillar 3 report but may be cross-referenced from statutory accounts.
