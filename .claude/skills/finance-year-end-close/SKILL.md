# Year-End Close Process

Extended close cycle (Day 1-30) building on month-end procedures with additional year-end-specific steps: inventory, provisions, impairment, going concern, post-BS events, dividends, final accruals, adjusting journals, and statutory accounts preparation. Dual-framework coverage for FRS 102 and IFRS throughout.

---

## 1. Year-End Close Timetable (Day 1-30)

### Phase 1: Standard Month-End (Days 1-10)
Execute the full month-end close process (revenue cut-off, accruals, prepayments, depreciation, bank recs, intercompany, payroll). All month-end steps per the finance-month-end-close skill apply here as the foundation.

### Phase 2: Year-End Specific (Days 11-20)

**Day 11-12 -- Inventory Count and Reconciliation**
- Attend/observe physical inventory count (or cycle count reconciliation if perpetual system)
- Reconcile count sheets to inventory sub-ledger; investigate and post count adjustments
- Assess NRV: compare cost to estimated selling price less costs to complete and sell (IAS 2 / FRS 102 S13)
- Write down obsolete, slow-moving, or damaged stock; document basis of write-down

**Day 13-14 -- Provisions: Create, Review, Release**
- Review all existing provisions against IAS 37 / FRS 102 S21 criteria: present obligation from past event, probable outflow, reliable estimate
- Legal provisions: obtain legal counsel's assessment of probable liability and range
- Restructuring provisions: confirm detailed formal plan announced to affected parties (IAS 37.72)
- Dilapidation provisions: update estimate based on surveyor reports and remaining lease term
- Onerous contracts: identify contracts where unavoidable costs exceed economic benefits
- Release provisions no longer required; document rationale for each movement

**Day 15 -- Impairment Indicators Review**
- IAS 36 / FRS 102 S27: assess indicators for each CGU -- declining revenue, market changes, obsolescence, increased discount rates, carrying amount exceeds market capitalisation
- If indicators present: perform impairment test (recoverable amount = higher of fair value less costs of disposal and value in use)
- Goodwill and indefinite-life intangibles: test annually regardless of indicators (IFRS only; FRS 102 amortises goodwill)
- Document conclusion per CGU even if no impairment identified

**Day 16 -- Going Concern Assessment**
- Prepare cash flow forecasts for at least 12 months from date of signing the accounts
- Stress-test forecasts: downside scenarios, sensitivity analysis on key assumptions
- Review covenant compliance: current and forecast periods
- Assess available facilities: committed vs uncommitted, maturity dates, refinancing risk
- Document management's conclusion: going concern basis appropriate / material uncertainty exists / going concern basis not appropriate

**Day 17 -- Post-Balance Sheet Events Review (IAS 10 / FRS 102 S32)**
- Adjusting events (conditions existed at BS date): adjust the financial statements -- e.g., resolution of court case, bankruptcy of debtor, discovery of fraud, sale of inventory below cost confirming NRV write-down
- Non-adjusting events (conditions arose after BS date): disclose nature and estimated financial effect -- e.g., major acquisition, natural disaster, share issue, restructuring announcement
- Review period: from BS date through to date of board approval of accounts
- Maintain a post-BS events log; update through signing date

**Day 18 -- Dividends and Distributable Reserves**
- Proposed vs declared: under FRS 102, dividends declared after BS date are non-adjusting events (disclose only); dividends declared before BS date are liabilities
- IFRS (IAS 10.12-13): dividends declared after reporting period are not recognised as liabilities
- Calculate distributable reserves per CA 2006 s830-s831: accumulated realised profits less accumulated realised losses (relevant accounts basis)
- For interim dividends: directors must be satisfied company has sufficient distributable reserves (CA 2006 s836)
- Unrealised gains in revaluation reserve are not distributable; share premium is not distributable (unless court-approved capital reduction)

### Phase 3: Final Adjustments and Lock (Days 19-25)

**Day 19-20 -- Final Accruals**
- Audit fee accrual: obtain engagement letter or fee estimate from auditors
- Corporation tax provision: calculate current tax liability using enacted/substantively enacted rate; reconcile ETR to standard rate (tax reconciliation)
- Bonus true-up: finalise annual bonus pool based on actual performance vs targets; adjust accrual
- Holiday pay accrual: calculate untaken holiday entitlement at year-end x daily rate

**Day 21-22 -- Year-End Adjusting Journals**
- All adjusting journals require: journal description, preparer, authoriser, supporting documentation
- Authority levels: define approval thresholds (e.g., journals over GBP 50k require FC approval, over GBP 250k require FD)
- Types: reclassification journals, elimination journals, error correction, estimate adjustments
- Dual sign-off: no journal posted without independent review

**Day 23-24 -- Trial Balance Lock and Period Close Controls**
- Run final adjusted TB; agree to prior year comparatives on a line-by-line basis
- Lock the period in the ERP/accounting system (prevent further postings without FC override)
- Generate period close report: list of all journals posted after Day 10, with authorisation trail
- Archive all supporting schedules and reconciliations

**Day 25 -- Statutory Accounts Drafting Timeline**
- Issue accounts drafting timetable to all contributors (tax, legal, company secretary, auditors)
- Prepare first draft of primary statements from locked TB
- Begin notes drafting (see finance-notes-disclosures skill)

### Phase 4: Audit and Approval (Days 26-30)

**Day 26-28 -- Audit Fieldwork Support**
- Provide auditors with TB, management accounts, all reconciliations, and schedules
- Respond to audit queries within agreed SLA (typically 48 hours)
- Post audit adjustments (agreed); track unadjusted differences

**Day 29-30 -- Board Approval and Filing**
- Present final accounts to Audit Committee / Board for approval
- Directors sign the balance sheet and directors' report
- File with Companies House: private companies within 9 months of year-end, public within 6 months
- File corporation tax return with HMRC within 12 months of period end

## 2. Provisions Detail (IAS 37 / FRS 102 S21)

| Provision type | Recognition test | Measurement | Review frequency |
|---|---|---|---|
| Legal claims | Probable adverse outcome | Best estimate of settlement | Each reporting date |
| Restructuring | Detailed formal plan + announced | Direct expenditure only (not ongoing) | Each reporting date |
| Dilapidations | Lease obligation to restore | Discounted estimate (if material) | Annually with surveyor |
| Onerous contracts | Unavoidable costs > benefits | Lower of cost to fulfil and cost to exit | Each reporting date |
| Warranty | Obligation from past sales | Expected value or most likely amount | Each reporting date |

FRS 102 S21 mirrors IAS 37 closely. Key difference: FRS 102 does not require discounting unless the effect is material and the time value of money is significant.

## 3. Going Concern -- Key Documentation

- 12-month+ cash flow forecast from signing date (not from BS date)
- Bank facility letters: committed amounts, expiry dates, covenants
- Covenant compliance calculations: actual and forecast
- Sensitivity analysis: revenue decline, cost increases, debtor collection delays
- Mitigating actions available: asset sales, cost reductions, additional funding
- Management's formal written assessment and conclusion

## 4. FS-Specific Year-End Considerations

**Actuarial Valuations (Insurance / Pensions)**: obtain actuary's report for defined benefit schemes (IAS 19 / FRS 102 S28); insurance entities require actuarial sign-off on technical provisions (claims reserves, IBNR). Reconcile actuarial figures to GL.

**Capital Adequacy Calculations**: banks -- CET1, Tier 1, Total Capital ratios (CRR/CRD); insurance -- Solvency Capital Requirement and Minimum Capital Requirement (Solvency II); investment firms -- IFPR own funds requirement (MIFIDPRU). Document headroom above minimum requirements.

**Year-End Regulatory Returns**: banks -- COREP, FINREP, large exposures, liquidity returns (LCR, NSFR); insurance -- annual Solvency II QRTs, SFCR; FCA-regulated firms -- FIN returns (RMAR, FSA returns). Reconcile regulatory returns to audited accounts; document and explain any differences.
