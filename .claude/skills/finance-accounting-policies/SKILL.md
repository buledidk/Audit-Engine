# Accounting Policies -- Selection, Drafting & Maintenance

This skill covers the complete lifecycle of accounting policies: selecting the right policies under FRS 102 or IFRS, drafting the policy notes for the financial statements, managing changes and corrections, preparing technical accounting memoranda for complex transactions, and handling first-time adoption transitions.

---

## 1. Policy Selection Framework

### 1.1 FRS 102 Policy Selection
FRS 102 provides a hierarchy for selecting accounting policies (Section 10.4):
1. Apply the specific requirement of the relevant section of FRS 102
2. If no specific requirement, look to the requirements and guidance in FRS 102 dealing with similar and related issues
3. Then consider the definitions, recognition criteria, and measurement concepts in Section 2 (Concepts and Pervasive Principles)

Key policy choices available under FRS 102:
- **Investment property** (S16): fair value through P&L vs cost model
- **PPE** (S17): cost model vs revaluation model
- **Borrowing costs** (S25): expense as incurred vs capitalise (qualifying assets)
- **Government grants** (S24): performance model vs accrual model
- **Financial instruments**: S11 (basic) and S12 (other) vs IAS 39/IFRS 9 option (S11.2)
- **Hedge accounting**: may elect to apply S12 hedge accounting or IAS 39 hedge accounting (S12.2)

### 1.2 IFRS Policy Selection
IAS 8 governs accounting policy selection. Where IFRS provides a choice:
- **PPE** (IAS 16): cost model vs revaluation model
- **Investment property** (IAS 40): fair value model vs cost model
- **Intangibles** (IAS 38): cost model vs revaluation model (rare in practice)
- **Inventory cost formulas** (IAS 2): FIFO vs weighted average (LIFO prohibited)
- **Borrowing costs** (IAS 23): capitalisation is mandatory for qualifying assets (no choice -- must capitalise)
- **Comprehensive income presentation** (IAS 1): single statement vs two statements (P&L + OCI)
- **Cash flow** (IAS 7): direct vs indirect method; interest/dividends classification

### 1.3 Consistency Requirements
- Once selected, a policy must be applied consistently to all similar transactions (IAS 8.13 / FRS 102 S10.7)
- Policy changes are permitted only if required by a new standard or if the change results in more reliable and relevant information
- Voluntary changes require retrospective application with restatement of comparatives

## 2. Drafting Accounting Policy Notes

### 2.1 Structure of the Accounting Policies Note
The accounting policies note is typically Note 1 (or Note 2 after the General Information note). Structure:

1. **Basis of preparation** -- which framework (FRS 102, IFRS, FRS 105), going concern basis, historical cost convention (with exceptions), functional and presentational currency
2. **Significant accounting policies** -- one sub-section per material policy area:
   - Revenue recognition
   - Property, plant and equipment
   - Intangible assets
   - Financial instruments
   - Inventories
   - Leases
   - Employee benefits (pensions, share-based payments)
   - Taxation (current and deferred)
   - Provisions
   - Foreign currencies
   - Government grants
   - Investment property
3. **Significant judgements and estimation uncertainty** (FRS 102 S8.6-8.7 / IAS 1.122-133)

### 2.2 Drafting Process
1. Obtain the prior year policies as the starting point
2. Review all new transactions in the period -- do any require a new policy or amendment to an existing one?
3. Review new standards, amendments, and interpretations effective for the period
4. Draft or update each policy in plain, entity-specific language (avoid copying standard text verbatim without tailoring)
5. Cross-reference each policy to the relevant standard section
6. Review for internal consistency (e.g., if you state cost model for PPE, do not refer to revaluation elsewhere)
7. Financial Controller review and sign-off
8. Auditor review (policies are a key area of audit focus)

### 2.3 Common Drafting Pitfalls
- Boilerplate text that does not reflect the entity's actual transactions
- Including policies for transaction types the entity does not have
- Omitting policies for material transaction types
- Failing to update for new standards (e.g., still referencing IAS 17 when IFRS 16 applies)
- Not disclosing the specific judgements made (e.g., lease classification, revenue timing)

## 3. Policy Changes, Corrections & Estimates (IAS 8 / FRS 102 S10)

### 3.1 Change in Accounting Policy
- **Mandatory change**: new standard requires it (e.g., adoption of IFRS 16)
- **Voluntary change**: entity elects to change for more reliable/relevant information
- Treatment: retrospective application -- restate opening balances and comparatives as if the new policy had always applied
- Disclose: nature of change, reason, amount of adjustment to each line item, amount of adjustment to EPS (IFRS)
- Impracticability exemption: if retrospective application is impracticable, apply prospectively from the earliest practicable date

### 3.2 Prior Period Errors (FRS 102 S10.19-10.23 / IAS 8.41-49)
- Errors in recognition, measurement, disclosure, or presentation in prior period financial statements
- Treatment: retrospective restatement -- correct opening balances and comparatives
- Disclose: nature of error, amount of correction for each line item, amount of correction to EPS (IFRS)
- Distinguish from changes in estimate (which are prospective)

### 3.3 Change in Accounting Estimate
- Adjustments arising from new information or new developments (e.g., revised useful life, updated bad debt provision rate)
- Treatment: prospective -- recognise the effect in the current and future periods
- Disclose: nature and amount of change; if impracticable to quantify, state that fact
- Examples: change in depreciation rate, change in ECL model assumptions, revision of warranty provision estimate

## 4. Technical Accounting Memoranda

### 4.1 When to Prepare a Technical Memo
- Complex or unusual transaction (business combination, restructuring, debt modification)
- New standard adoption or early adoption
- Ambiguous accounting treatment where judgement is required
- Significant estimation uncertainty
- Related party transactions with non-market terms
- Any transaction where the auditor is likely to challenge the treatment

### 4.2 Technical Memo Structure
1. **Transaction summary** -- what happened, parties involved, key terms and dates
2. **Applicable standards** -- list every relevant standard (primary and ancillary)
3. **Analysis** -- apply the standard requirements to the transaction facts; consider multiple interpretations if ambiguous
4. **Conclusion** -- recommended accounting treatment with journal entries
5. **Disclosure implications** -- what must be disclosed in the notes
6. **Quantification** -- amounts, measurement basis, any estimates involved
7. **Prepared by / reviewed by / date** -- sign-off trail

## 5. First-Time Adoption

### 5.1 FRS 102 Section 35 -- Transition
- Prepare an opening balance sheet at the date of transition (start of earliest comparative period)
- Apply FRS 102 recognition and measurement retrospectively, with mandatory and optional exemptions
- Key mandatory exceptions: derecognition of financial assets/liabilities, hedge accounting, estimates (use estimates made under previous GAAP)
- Key optional exemptions: business combinations (need not restate pre-transition combinations), fair value as deemed cost for PPE, share-based payments (need not apply to grants vested before transition)
- Reconciliations required: equity at transition date, equity at end of last period under previous GAAP, profit or loss for last period under previous GAAP

### 5.2 IFRS 1 -- First-Time Adoption of IFRS
- Opening IFRS balance sheet at the date of transition
- Apply IFRS retrospectively, subject to mandatory exceptions and voluntary exemptions
- Mandatory exceptions include: estimates, derecognition, hedge accounting, NCI measurement, classification of financial assets, embedded derivatives, government loans
- Voluntary exemptions include: business combinations, fair value/revaluation as deemed cost, leases, cumulative translation differences, compound instruments, employee benefits, share-based payments, insurance contracts (IFRS 17 transition)
- Reconciliations: equity under previous GAAP to IFRS at transition date and at end of last period under previous GAAP; total comprehensive income reconciliation

## 6. Financial Services Policy Considerations

### 6.1 Hedge Accounting Policy
- Must specify: type of hedging relationship (fair value, cash flow, net investment), risk being hedged, hedging instrument, hedged item, effectiveness assessment method
- FRS 102 S12 or IAS 39 election; under IFRS, entities may elect IFRS 9 hedge accounting or remain on IAS 39
- Documentation requirements: formal designation and documentation at inception of the hedge

### 6.2 Expected Credit Loss (ECL) Methodology
- IFRS 9 three-stage model: Stage 1 (12-month ECL), Stage 2 (lifetime ECL, not credit-impaired), Stage 3 (lifetime ECL, credit-impaired)
- Policy must specify: definition of default, significant increase in credit risk (SICR) criteria, forward-looking information incorporated, cure criteria, write-off policy
- FRS 102 S11: incurred loss model (simpler) unless entity has elected IFRS 9 treatment

### 6.3 Insurance Contracts Policy (IFRS 17 / FRS 103)
- IFRS 17: measurement model (general model, premium allocation approach, variable fee approach), contract boundaries, discount rates, risk adjustment methodology, CSM amortisation pattern
- FRS 103: permits continuation of existing accounting policies for insurance contracts with additional disclosure requirements
- Policy must address: claims provisions (IBNR, IBNER), premium recognition, acquisition costs, reinsurance
