# Group Consolidation -- Full Process Guide

This skill covers the end-to-end group consolidation process for preparing consolidated financial statements under both IFRS and FRS 102 (UK GAAP).

---

## 1. Consolidation Scope

### 1.1 Subsidiaries -- Full Consolidation
- IFRS 10: control exists when the investor has power over the investee, exposure to variable returns, and ability to use power to affect returns
- FRS 102 S9: subsidiary is an entity controlled by the parent; control is the power to govern financial and operating policies
- All subsidiaries must be consolidated unless the parent qualifies for an exemption (s401 CA 2006 for intermediate parents, s383 CA 2006 small group exemption)

### 1.2 Associates -- Equity Method
- IAS 28 / FRS 102 S14: significant influence (generally 20-50% voting rights, board representation, participation in policy-making, material transactions, interchange of managerial personnel, provision of essential technical information)
- Equity method: recognise investment at cost, adjusted for post-acquisition share of profit/loss and OCI, less dividends received

### 1.3 Joint Ventures and Joint Arrangements
- IFRS 11: classify as joint operation (recognise share of assets, liabilities, revenue, expenses) or joint venture (equity method)
- FRS 102 S15: jointly controlled entities accounted for using equity method or proportionate consolidation (policy choice)
- Joint control requires unanimous consent of parties sharing control

## 2. Step 1 -- Reporting Package Collection

### 2.1 Standard Reporting Pack Design
- Group reporting pack template covering: trial balance, balance sheet, income statement, cash flow data, intercompany schedules, related party schedules, segment data, accounting policy compliance certificate
- Uniform chart of accounts or mapping table from local COA to group COA
- Specify reporting currency, exchange rates to use, and reporting timetable

### 2.2 Timetable and Deadlines
- Issue reporting pack instructions 4-6 weeks before component year-end
- Hard deadlines for first submission, query resolution, and final lock-down
- Escalation protocol for late or incomplete submissions

---

## 3. Step 2 -- Accounting Policy Alignment Across Group

### 3.1 Group Accounting Policy Manual
- Maintain a group accounting policy manual covering all material areas (revenue, leases, financial instruments, provisions, pensions, share-based payments, taxation)
- Identify local GAAP to IFRS/FRS 102 differences and require adjusting journals in reporting packs
- Common alignment issues: revenue recognition timing, lease classification, pension measurement, government grants

### 3.2 Adjustment Journals
- Components submit GAAP adjustment journals converting local statutory accounts to group policies
- Group finance reviews and approves adjustments; maintain a log of recurring policy adjustments by entity

---

## 4. Step 3 -- Currency Translation (IAS 21 / FRS 102 S30)

### 4.1 Functional Currency Determination
- Functional currency is the currency of the primary economic environment in which the entity operates
- Indicators: currency of sales prices, labour/material costs, financing activities, receipts from operating activities
- Factual determination, not a policy choice

### 4.2 Translation of Foreign Operations
- Assets and liabilities: closing rate at the balance sheet date
- Income and expenses: average rate for the period (or transaction date rate if rates fluctuate significantly)
- Resulting exchange differences: recognised in OCI and accumulated in the foreign currency translation reserve (FCTR)
- Goodwill and FV adjustments on acquisition of a foreign operation treated as assets/liabilities of the foreign operation, translated at closing rate

### 4.3 Practical Considerations
- Source of exchange rates: use a single consistent source (Bank of England, ECB, Bloomberg)
- Hyper-inflationary economies: apply IAS 29 before translating (restate to current purchasing power, then translate at closing rate)
- Disposal of foreign operation: recycle cumulative FCTR to profit or loss

---

## 5. Step 4 -- Goodwill on Acquisition (IFRS 3 / FRS 102 S19)

### 5.1 Calculation
- Goodwill = Consideration transferred + NCI at acquisition + previously held equity interest (step acquisition) - Fair value of identifiable net assets acquired
- Consideration includes: cash, shares issued (FV at acquisition date), deferred consideration (FV), contingent consideration (FV, re-measured through P&L under IFRS 3)
- FRS 102 S19: goodwill = cost of combination - fair value of identifiable net assets; NCI at proportionate share (no full goodwill method)

### 5.2 Fair Value of Net Assets
- Identifiable assets and liabilities at fair value at acquisition date
- Recognise intangibles separately from goodwill: customer relationships, brands, technology, licences, favourable contracts
- Deferred tax on FV adjustments (IAS 12.66)
- Bargain purchase: excess of net assets over consideration recognised immediately in P&L (IFRS 3); reassess identification and measurement before recognising

### 5.3 Post-Acquisition Adjustments
- Measurement period: up to 12 months from acquisition date to finalise fair values (IFRS 3.45)
- Adjustments within measurement period are retrospective; after measurement period, recognised in current period P&L

---

## 6. Step 5 -- Intercompany Elimination

### 6.1 Balance Elimination
- Eliminate all IC receivables and payables (trading, loan, dividends, management charges)
- Residual difference after elimination = timing difference or error; investigate and resolve before finalising

### 6.2 Transaction Elimination
- Eliminate IC sales and purchases (revenue and cost of sales)
- Eliminate IC management charges, royalties, interest, and dividends

### 6.3 Unrealised Profit
- Inventory: eliminate unrealised profit on goods sold intra-group still held in inventory at year-end (Dr P&L/retained earnings, Cr Inventory)
- Fixed assets: eliminate unrealised profit on intra-group transfers; adjust depreciation in subsequent periods
- Upstream (sub to parent): adjust against NCI's share; downstream (parent to sub): full adjustment against parent

### 6.4 Dividends
- Eliminate dividends received by parent from subsidiaries (Dr dividend income, Cr dividends paid)
- Dividends paid to NCI remain in consolidated accounts (cash flow: financing activity)

---

## 7. Step 6 -- Non-Controlling Interest (NCI)

### 7.1 NCI at Acquisition
- IFRS 3: choice of measuring NCI at fair value (full goodwill) or proportionate share of net assets (partial goodwill) -- election per acquisition
- FRS 102 S19: NCI always at proportionate share of net assets

### 7.2 NCI at Reporting Date
- NCI share of post-acquisition retained earnings and reserves
- NCI share of current year profit or loss
- NCI share of OCI items (FX reserve, revaluation reserve, cash flow hedge reserve)
- Presented separately in equity on consolidated BS and as a separate line in consolidated income statement

### 7.3 Changes in Ownership without Loss of Control
- Treated as equity transactions (no gain/loss in P&L)

---

## 8. Step 7 -- Associates/JVs: Equity Method

### 8.1 Recognition and Measurement
- Recognise at cost of investment (including directly attributable transaction costs under FRS 102)
- Goodwill within the carrying amount of the investment (not separately recognised)
- Adjust carrying amount for investor's share of post-acquisition profit/loss and OCI; deduct dividends received

### 8.2 Impairment
- Assess for impairment indicators at each reporting date (IAS 36 / FRS 102 S27)
- Compare carrying amount to recoverable amount of the investment as a single asset

---

## 9. Step 8 -- Fair Value Adjustments

### 9.1 Unwinding FV Adjustments
- Additional depreciation on revalued PPE, amortisation of identified intangibles
- Deferred tax on FV adjustments: release as underlying temporary differences reverse

### 9.2 Alignment and Reclassification Journals
- Group-level adjustments for policy alignment issues not corrected at component level
- Reclassification journals for presentation consistency across the group

---

## 10. Step 9 -- Consolidation Journals

### 10.1 Journal Log
- Maintain a master journal log: journal number, description, entity, debit/credit, preparer, reviewer, supporting reference
- Distinguish between recurring journals (repeat each period) and non-recurring journals
- Categories: elimination entries, goodwill, NCI, FX, FV unwind, policy alignment

---

## 11. Step 10 -- Consolidated TB to Financial Statements

### 11.1 Consolidated TB
- Aggregate all component TBs (translated to presentation currency) plus all consolidation journals
- Consolidated TB should balance to zero and map directly to consolidated financial statements

### 11.2 Consolidated Financial Statements
- Consolidated statement of financial position (balance sheet)
- Consolidated statement of profit or loss and other comprehensive income
- Consolidated statement of changes in equity (SOCIE)
- Consolidated statement of cash flows
- Notes to the consolidated financial statements

---

## 12. Goodwill Impairment Review (IAS 36 / FRS 102 S27)

### 12.1 IFRS
- Goodwill is not amortised; test for impairment at least annually and whenever indicators exist
- Allocate goodwill to cash-generating units (CGUs) or groups of CGUs expected to benefit from synergies
- Recoverable amount: higher of fair value less costs of disposal and value in use (PV of future cash flows)

### 12.2 FRS 102
- Goodwill is amortised over its useful life (max 5 years if life cannot be reliably estimated, up to 10 years if it can)
- Test for impairment when indicators exist (indicator-based, not annual)

### 12.3 Sensitivity
- Disclose key assumptions and the amount by which they would need to change to trigger impairment

---

## 13. Group Cash Flow Statement

### 13.1 Preparation
- Prepare from consolidated BS movements, adjusted for non-cash items and consolidation adjustments
- Operating: consolidated profit, add back depreciation/amortisation, working capital movements
- Investing: acquisitions (net of cash acquired), disposals (net of cash disposed), capex
- Financing: borrowings, dividends to parent shareholders, dividends to NCI, lease payments (IFRS 16), share issues/buybacks

### 13.2 Elimination
- Eliminate IC dividends from investing/financing activities
- Separate NCI cash flows: dividends paid to NCI as financing activity

---

## 14. Financial Services Specific Considerations

### 14.1 Banking Group
- SPV consolidation (IFRS 10): securitisation vehicles, covered bond programmes, conduits assessed for control
- Regulatory consolidation scope (CRR) may differ from accounting consolidation -- reconcile differences
- Structured entities must be consolidated if the bank controls them regardless of ownership percentage

### 14.2 Insurance Group
- Solvency II group supervision: consolidation of insurance and reinsurance undertakings, holding companies, ancillary service undertakings
- Group SCR calculation: consolidation method or deduction-and-aggregation method
- Intra-group transactions and risk concentrations reporting to supervisor

### 14.3 Fund Consolidation
- Investment entities exemption (IFRS 10.27): investment entities measure subsidiaries at FVTPL instead of consolidating
- Fund-of-funds: assess whether the parent fund controls underlying funds
- GP/LP structures: assess control by the general partner, considering substantive removal rights of LPs
