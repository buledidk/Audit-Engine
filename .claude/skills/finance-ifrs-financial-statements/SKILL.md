# IFRS Financial Statement Preparation

Complete guide to preparing financial statements under IFRS as issued by the IASB (and as adopted by the UK where applicable). Covers IAS 1 presentation, each primary statement, notes, first-time adoption, segment reporting, EPS, interim reporting, and FS-sector overlays.

---

## 1. IAS 1 -- Presentation of Financial Statements

### Complete Set (IAS 1.10)
1. Statement of Financial Position as at the end of the period
2. Statement of Profit or Loss and Other Comprehensive Income for the period
3. Statement of Changes in Equity for the period
4. Statement of Cash Flows for the period
5. Notes (significant material accounting policy information plus explanatory)
6. Statement of Financial Position at the beginning of the earliest comparative period if retrospective restatement applies

### General Features
- **Fair presentation and compliance with IFRS**: explicit and unreserved statement of compliance required (IAS 1.16)
- **Going concern**: assess at least 12 months from the reporting date; disclose material uncertainties
- **Accrual basis**: recognise items when they meet the Framework definitions
- **Materiality and aggregation**: each material class of similar items presented separately; immaterial items may be aggregated
- **Offsetting**: prohibited unless required or permitted by an IFRS (e.g., IAS 32 for financial instruments)
- **Frequency of reporting**: at least annually; if period changes, disclose reason and non-comparability
- **Comparative information**: minimum one comparative period for all amounts; narrative comparatives where relevant
- **Consistency of presentation**: retain classification and presentation period to period unless a standard requires change or reclassification better reflects the entity's operations

## 2. Statement of Financial Position

### Current/Non-Current Distinction (IAS 1.60-76)
- **Current assets**: expected to be realised within 12 months or the operating cycle, held primarily for trading, or cash/cash equivalents (unless restricted)
- **Current liabilities**: expected to be settled within 12 months or the operating cycle, held primarily for trading, or entity has no unconditional right to defer settlement beyond 12 months
- Non-current: everything else
- Entities may present in order of liquidity if this provides more reliable and relevant information (common in banking/financial services)

### Minimum Line Items (IAS 1.54)
- PPE, investment property, intangible assets, financial assets, investments (equity method), biological assets, inventories, trade and other receivables, cash and equivalents, trade and other payables, provisions, financial liabilities, current tax, deferred tax, non-controlling interests, issued capital and reserves

### Order of Liquidity (FS Entities)
- Banks and financial institutions typically present the balance sheet in order of liquidity (most liquid first) rather than current/non-current
- Assets: cash, loans and advances to banks, trading assets, derivative assets, loans to customers, investment securities, PPE
- Liabilities: deposits from banks, customer deposits, trading liabilities, derivative liabilities, debt securities issued, subordinated liabilities, equity

## 3. Statement of Profit or Loss and OCI

### Presentation Options
- Single statement: all items of income and expense, including OCI, in one statement
- Two statements: separate income statement (to profit/loss) plus OCI statement (starting from profit/loss)

### Classification of Expenses
- **By nature**: raw materials, employee costs, depreciation, amortisation, other expenses -- simpler, more objective
- **By function**: cost of sales, distribution, administration, other -- more informative but requires allocation judgement; if used, must disclose depreciation, amortisation, and employee costs by nature in notes

### Other Comprehensive Income Items
Items that will subsequently be reclassified to profit or loss (recycling):
- FX translation differences on foreign operations (IAS 21)
- Cash flow hedging gains/losses (IFRS 9)
- Reclassification adjustments on disposal of foreign operations

Items that will not be reclassified (non-recycling):
- Revaluation surplus on PPE (IAS 16) and intangibles (IAS 38)
- Remeasurements of defined benefit plans (IAS 19)
- Fair value changes on equity instruments designated at FVOCI (IFRS 9)
- Own credit risk changes on financial liabilities at FVTPL (IFRS 9)

### Tax on OCI
- Disclose income tax relating to each component of OCI either on the face or in notes (IAS 1.90-91)

## 4. Statement of Changes in Equity

For each component of equity, present:
- Total comprehensive income for the period (attributable to owners and NCI separately)
- Effects of retrospective application or retrospective restatement (IAS 8)
- Reconciliation of carrying amount at beginning and end: profit/loss, OCI, transactions with owners (contributions and distributions), changes in ownership interests in subsidiaries that do not result in loss of control

Components typically: share capital, share premium, treasury shares, retained earnings, revaluation surplus, hedging reserve, translation reserve, FVOCI reserve, NCI.

## 5. Statement of Cash Flows (IAS 7)

### Direct vs Indirect Method
- **Indirect** (most common): start with profit/loss, adjust for non-cash items and working capital changes
- **Direct**: report major classes of gross cash receipts and payments (cash received from customers, cash paid to suppliers, etc.) -- encouraged by IAS 7 but rarely used in practice

### Classification
- **Operating**: principal revenue-producing activities; also includes items not investing or financing
- **Investing**: acquisition/disposal of long-term assets and other investments
- **Financing**: activities that result in changes in equity capital and borrowings

### Interest and Dividends (IAS 7.33-34 -- classification choice)
- Interest paid: operating or financing (state policy and apply consistently)
- Interest received: operating or investing
- Dividends paid: operating or financing
- Dividends received: operating or investing

### Non-Cash Transactions
- Exclude from the cash flow statement but disclose in notes (e.g., acquisition of asset by lease, conversion of debt to equity)

## 6. Notes Structure

Ordering convention per IAS 1.114:
1. Statement of compliance with IFRS
2. Summary of material accounting policies (IAS 1.117-124): measurement basis, specific policies relevant to understanding the financial statements; IFRS Practice Statement 2 on making materiality judgements applies
3. Supporting information for items in the primary statements (in order they appear)
4. Other disclosures: contingent liabilities, unrecognised contractual commitments, non-financial disclosures

Critical judgements and key estimation uncertainty (IAS 1.122-133): disclose judgements that have the most significant effect on amounts recognised, and assumptions about the future with a significant risk of material adjustment within the next year.

## 7. First-Time Adoption (IFRS 1)

### Process
1. Select the date of transition (beginning of earliest comparative period)
2. Prepare an opening IFRS balance sheet at that date
3. Apply IFRS retrospectively, subject to mandatory exceptions and voluntary exemptions
4. Prepare reconciliations: equity at transition date, equity at end of last GAAP period, total comprehensive income for last GAAP period

### Mandatory Exceptions (cannot avoid)
- Estimates: use estimates consistent with previous GAAP (do not use hindsight)
- Derecognition of financial assets/liabilities: apply prospectively from transition
- Hedge accounting: apply IFRS 9 hedge requirements prospectively
- Non-controlling interests: apply IAS 27/IFRS 10 requirements prospectively

### Key Voluntary Exemptions (choose to apply)
- Business combinations: do not restate acquisitions before transition date (use previous GAAP carrying amounts)
- Deemed cost: use fair value or revaluation as deemed cost for PPE, investment property, intangibles
- Cumulative translation differences: reset to zero at transition date
- Leases: apply IFRS 16 using modified retrospective approach at transition date
- Employee benefits: recognise all cumulative actuarial gains/losses at transition

## 8. Segment Reporting (IFRS 8)

- Applies to entities with listed debt or equity (or in process of listing)
- Identify **operating segments**: components reviewed by the Chief Operating Decision Maker (CODM) for resource allocation and performance assessment
- **Aggregation criteria**: segments may be combined if they have similar economic characteristics and are similar in nature of products/services, production processes, customer type, distribution methods, and regulatory environment
- Disclose per segment: revenue (external and inter-segment), measure of profit/loss, total assets (if regularly provided to CODM), total liabilities (if regularly provided), and specified items (depreciation, additions to non-current assets, interest income/expense, tax)
- Entity-wide disclosures: revenue by product/service, by geography, and by major customer (if 10%+ of total revenue)

---

## 9. Earnings Per Share (IAS 33)

- Applies to entities with listed ordinary shares or in process of listing
- **Basic EPS**: profit/loss attributable to ordinary equity holders / weighted average number of ordinary shares outstanding during the period
- Weighted average: adjust for shares issued, bought back, bonus issues; bonus issues are treated as if they had been outstanding for all periods presented
- **Diluted EPS**: adjust numerator for effects of dilutive potential ordinary shares (options, warrants, convertibles); adjust denominator for additional shares that would be issued
- Only instruments that reduce EPS (or increase loss per share) are dilutive; anti-dilutive instruments are excluded
- Present on the face of the income statement for continuing operations and for total; discontinued operations EPS in notes or on face

---

## 10. Interim Reporting (IAS 34)

- Not mandatory under IFRS (but may be required by local regulation or listing rules)
- **Condensed financial statements**: condensed balance sheet, condensed income statement and OCI, condensed SOCIE, condensed cash flow, selected explanatory notes
- Same accounting policies as annual statements; discrete period approach (each interim is a standalone reporting period, not an allocation of expected annual results)
- Explanatory notes: significant events and transactions, changes in estimates, segment information, dividends, issuances/repurchases, contingencies, subsequent events

---

## 11. FS-Specific Considerations

**IFRS 17 Presentation (Insurance)**: insurance revenue replaces premium income; insurance service result presented separately from investment result; contractual service margin (CSM) shown on balance sheet; specific OCI treatment for insurance finance income/expense.

**IFRS 9 Reclassification Disclosures (Banking/FS)**: if financial assets are reclassified between FVTPL, FVOCI, and amortised cost, disclose date, amount, reason, and effect on financial statements. Staging disclosures: breakdown of financial assets by ECL stage (Stage 1, 2, 3) with gross carrying amount and loss allowance.

**Regulatory Capital Disclosures**: Pillar 3 disclosures for banks (CRR/CRD): CET1, AT1, Tier 2 capital, risk-weighted assets, capital ratios. MIFIDPRU for investment firms. Solvency II for insurers (SCR, MCR, own funds). These are separate from IFRS accounts but often cross-referenced.
