# Directors' Report and Strategic Report — Full Process Guide

This skill covers the preparation, review, and approval of the directors' report and strategic report for UK companies under the Companies Act 2006, including enhanced requirements for large, quoted, and public interest entities.

---

## 1. Directors' Report (CA 2006 s415-s419)

### 1.1 Mandatory Contents for All Companies
- Names of all persons who were directors during the financial year (s416(1)(a))
- Statement of dividends: recommended final dividend and any interim dividends paid
- Political donations and expenditure (if exceeding GBP 200 in aggregate) (s366-s367)
- Qualifying third-party and directors' indemnity provisions (s236)
- Statement of directors' responsibilities for preparing financial statements
- Disclosure of financial instruments and their risk management policies (s417(4) for medium/large)
- Post balance sheet events affecting the company's position
- Likely future developments of the company and its subsidiaries
- Research and development activities during the year
- Branches outside the UK (s416(3))
- Employee involvement and disability policy (companies with 250+ employees) (s416(4))

### 1.2 Small Company Exemptions (s415A)
- Small companies may omit the strategic report entirely
- Directors' report for small companies need only contain: directors' names, dividends, and the s415A exemption statement
- The exemption statement reads: "This report has been prepared in accordance with the provisions applicable to companies entitled to the small companies exemption"
- Qualifying thresholds (CA 2006 s382): two of three — turnover <=10.2m, balance sheet total <=5.1m, employees <=50

### 1.3 Medium-Sized Company Requirements
- Must prepare both directors' report and strategic report
- May use abbreviated strategic report (omit non-financial KPIs)
- Qualifying thresholds (s465): two of three — turnover <=36m, balance sheet total <=18m, employees <=250

### 1.4 Additional Disclosures for Large/Quoted Companies
- Statement on engagement with employees (s414CZA)
- Statement on engagement with suppliers, customers, and others (s414CZB)
- Disclosure of greenhouse gas emissions (large companies and LLPs under SECR)
- Statement of corporate governance arrangements (unlisted large companies under The Companies (Miscellaneous Reporting) Regulations 2018)

## 2. Strategic Report (CA 2006 s414A-s414D)

### 2.1 Purpose and Scope
- Required for all companies other than those qualifying as small (s414A)
- Purpose: inform shareholders and help them assess how directors have performed their duty under s172 (s414C(1))
- Must be approved by the board and signed on behalf of the board (s414D)

### 2.2 Core Content — Business Review
- Fair review of the company's business and description of principal risks and uncertainties (s414C(2))
- Analysis using financial KPIs (all companies) and non-financial KPIs where appropriate (large companies)
- Balanced and comprehensive analysis consistent with the size and complexity of the business
- Description of the company's strategy and business model (large companies)
- Trends and factors likely to affect future development, performance, and position
- Environmental matters (including impact on the environment), employee matters, social and community matters, respect for human rights, and anti-corruption and anti-bribery matters

### 2.3 Section 172(1) Statement
- Required for all companies that are not small (The Companies (Miscellaneous Reporting) Regulations 2018)
- Must describe how directors have had regard to the matters set out in s172(1)(a)-(f):
  - (a) Likely consequences of any decision in the long term
  - (b) Interests of the company's employees
  - (c) Need to foster business relationships with suppliers, customers, and others
  - (d) Impact of operations on the community and environment
  - (e) Desirability of maintaining high standards of business conduct
  - (f) Need to act fairly as between members of the company
- Practical approach: describe principal decisions taken during the year and how stakeholder interests were considered

### 2.4 Principal Risks and Uncertainties
- Identify and describe the principal risks facing the company
- Explain how risks are managed or mitigated
- Categories typically include: financial risk (credit, liquidity, market, interest rate, FX), operational risk, regulatory risk, technology/cyber risk, people risk, reputational risk, climate-related risk
- Going concern and viability statement linkage (UK Corporate Governance Code for premium-listed)

## 3. Enhanced Strategic Report — Large Companies

### 3.1 Environmental, Social, and Governance (ESG)
- Large companies must include analysis of environmental matters, employees, social matters, human rights, and anti-corruption
- If the company has no policy on any of those matters, it must state that fact (s414CB(2))
- Climate-related financial disclosures: align with TCFD recommendations for in-scope entities

### 3.2 Gender Pay Gap Reporting
- Mandatory for employers with 250+ employees (Equality Act 2010 (Gender Pay Gap Information) Regulations 2017)
- Report: mean and median gender pay gap, mean and median bonus gap, proportion of men and women receiving bonus, proportion of men and women in each pay quartile
- Published on company website and government portal annually

### 3.3 Non-Financial Information Statement (NFIS)
- Required for PIEs with 500+ employees (s414CB)
- Must include description of policies (with due diligence processes), outcomes, principal risks, and KPIs relating to: environmental, employee, social/community, human rights, anti-corruption/bribery matters
- Description of the business model
- Cross-referencing to other sections of the annual report is permitted

## 4. Energy and Carbon Reporting

### 4.1 Streamlined Energy and Carbon Reporting (SECR)
- Applies to quoted companies, large unquoted companies, and large LLPs
- Must disclose: UK energy use (kWh), associated GHG emissions (tCO2e), at least one intensity ratio, methodology used, prior year comparatives
- Scope: Scope 1 (direct) and Scope 2 (electricity) emissions as a minimum
- Exemption for low energy users (consuming <40,000 kWh)

### 4.2 Task Force on Climate-related Financial Disclosures (TCFD)
- Mandatory for premium-listed companies, large UK-authorised asset managers, and large FCA-regulated asset owners (from April 2022)
- Four pillars: Governance, Strategy, Risk Management, Metrics and Targets
- Companies must disclose on a "comply or explain" basis
- Scenario analysis required (including a scenario consistent with limiting warming to 1.5-2 degrees)
- Transition to ISSB (IFRS S1/S2) is underway from 2025 onwards; UK endorsement expected via UK SRS

## 5. Corporate Governance Statement

### 5.1 Listed Companies — DTR 7.2
- Premium-listed companies must include a corporate governance statement in the directors' report (or standalone)
- Must reference the code applied (UK Corporate Governance Code) and explain departures ("comply or explain")
- Description of internal control and risk management systems relating to financial reporting
- Composition and operation of board and committees (audit, remuneration, nomination)
- Diversity policy

### 5.2 Unlisted Large Companies — Wates Principles
- The Companies (Miscellaneous Reporting) Regulations 2018 require large private companies to disclose their corporate governance arrangements
- May apply the Wates Corporate Governance Principles for Large Private Companies or another recognised code
- Disclose on company website and reference in directors' report

## 6. Approval, Signing, and Filing

### 6.1 Board Approval
- Strategic report and directors' report must be approved by the board of directors (s414D, s419)
- Approval is typically evidenced by board minutes
- The strategic report is signed by a director or the company secretary on behalf of the board (s414D(1))
- The directors' report is signed by a director or the company secretary (s419(1))

### 6.2 Filing Requirements
- Filed with Companies House as part of the annual accounts
- Filing deadline: 9 months after year-end (private), 6 months (public)
- Late filing penalties: GBP 150 to GBP 7,500 (private), GBP 750 to GBP 7,500 (public)
- Quoted companies must also publish the annual report on their website

## 7. Common Errors and FRC Findings

### 7.1 FRC Annual Review of Reporting — Recurring Themes
- Generic, boilerplate s172(1) statements lacking company-specific examples of principal decisions
- Inconsistency between principal risks described in the strategic report and accounting estimates/judgements in the notes
- Failure to link financial KPIs back to the financial statements
- Alternative performance measures (APMs) presented without clear reconciliation to GAAP/IFRS measures
- Inadequate going concern and viability statement disclosures (especially cash flow scenarios and reverse stress testing)
- Climate-related disclosures lacking quantitative metrics or scenario analysis
- Missing or incomplete SECR disclosures (especially Scope 2 and intensity ratios)
- Directors' report omitting required disclosures on post balance sheet events or future developments

### 7.2 Best Practice Recommendations
- Use a disclosure checklist mapped to CA 2006, FRS 102/IFRS, DTR, and UK Corporate Governance Code
- Cross-reference narrative in the front half to numbers in the back half
- Ensure consistency between the strategic report, risk disclosures, accounting judgements, and audit committee report
- Engage legal counsel on s172(1) statement drafting for sensitive stakeholder decisions

## 8. Financial Services Specific Considerations

### 8.1 Banking — Pillar 3 Disclosures
- CRR Article 431-455 requires disclosure of risk management, capital adequacy, leverage ratio, liquidity, remuneration
- Published separately or as part of the annual report
- Must be verified by senior management and, where appropriate, independently reviewed

### 8.2 Banking — Remuneration Report
- PRA Remuneration Rules and CRD require disclosure of remuneration policies, quantitative aggregate data, and identified staff (Material Risk Takers)
- Gender pay analysis within remuneration disclosures
- Malus and clawback provisions disclosure

### 8.3 Insurance — Solvency and Financial Condition Report (SFCR)
- Solvency II Directive requires annual publication of SFCR
- Contents: business and performance, system of governance, risk profile, valuation for solvency purposes, capital management
- Subject to external audit (specified sections)

### 8.4 Investment Firms — MIFIDPRU 8 Disclosure
- Investment firms subject to MIFIDPRU must publish disclosures on governance, own funds, own funds requirements, remuneration policy and practices
- Annual publication, on the firm's website, within the same timeframe as annual accounts
