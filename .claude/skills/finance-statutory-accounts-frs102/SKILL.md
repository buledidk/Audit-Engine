# FRS 102 Statutory Financial Statements

Complete guide to preparing statutory financial statements under FRS 102 (The Financial Reporting Standard applicable in the UK and Republic of Ireland), incorporating Companies Act 2006 requirements, filing obligations, and FS-sector overlays.

---

## 1. Complete Set of Financial Statements (FRS 102 S3.17)

A complete set comprises:
1. Statement of Financial Position (balance sheet) as at the reporting date
2. Either a single Statement of Comprehensive Income, or an Income Statement and a separate Statement of Comprehensive Income
3. Statement of Changes in Equity for the reporting period
4. Statement of Cash Flows for the reporting period (exempt for small companies qualifying under S1A)
5. Notes to the financial statements (including accounting policies and explanatory information)

---

## 2. Statement of Financial Position

### CA 2006 Balance Sheet Formats (SI 2008/410 Schedule 1)
- **Format 1 (vertical)**: assets less liabilities, most common in UK practice. Fixed assets, current assets, creditors due within one year, net current assets, total assets less current liabilities, creditors due after one year, provisions, net assets, capital and reserves
- **Format 2 (horizontal)**: assets on one side, liabilities and equity on the other; rarely used in practice

### Key Classification Rules
- Fixed assets: intangible (goodwill, development costs, concessions/patents), tangible (land/buildings, plant/machinery, fixtures, assets under construction), investments
- Current assets: stocks, debtors (split amounts due within and after one year), investments, cash at bank and in hand
- Creditors: amounts falling due within one year (trade creditors, tax, social security, accruals, deferred income, bank loans/overdrafts) and after more than one year
- Provisions for liabilities: deferred tax, other provisions (legal, dilapidation, restructuring)
- Capital and reserves: called-up share capital, share premium, revaluation reserve, other reserves, profit and loss account

### Comparative Figures
- Prior year comparatives required for every line item (CA 2006 s396, FRS 102 S3.14)
- If prior year is restated (error correction or policy change), disclose three balance sheets: current, prior (restated), beginning of prior period

## 3. Statement of Comprehensive Income

### Single-Statement Approach
Revenue through to profit/loss for the financial year, then other comprehensive income items, then total comprehensive income. OCI items under FRS 102: revaluation gains/losses on PPE (S17) and intangibles (S18), actuarial gains/losses on defined benefit plans (S28), FX gains/losses on translation of foreign operations (S30), gains/losses on hedging instruments in a cash flow hedge (S12).

### Two-Statement Approach
- Income Statement: revenue through to profit/loss for the financial year
- Statement of Comprehensive Income: opens with profit/loss, then OCI items, then total comprehensive income

### CA 2006 P&L Formats
- **Format 1 (by function)**: turnover, cost of sales, gross profit, distribution costs, administrative expenses, operating profit
- **Format 2 (by nature)**: turnover, change in stocks, own work capitalised, raw materials, staff costs, depreciation, other charges

### Exceptional Items
FRS 102 does not define "exceptional" but CA 2006 s396A requires disclosure of material items by nature and amount. Present within the relevant line item or as a separate line; disclose in notes.

### Discontinued Operations
FRS 102 S5.7D: disclose revenue, expenses, pre-tax profit/loss, and tax for discontinued operations separately.

---

## 4. Statement of Changes in Equity

Present for each component: share capital, share premium account, revaluation reserve, hedging reserve, profit and loss account (retained earnings), and any other reserves. Show: total comprehensive income, effects of retrospective application or restatement, dividends paid and declared, shares issued, treasury shares, and any other changes.

---

## 5. Statement of Cash Flows (FRS 102 S7)

### Indirect Method (standard UK practice)
- **Operating activities**: profit/loss for the year, adjust for non-cash items (depreciation, amortisation, impairment, loss/gain on disposal, share-based payments, movement in provisions), adjust for working capital changes (stocks, debtors, creditors), interest paid, tax paid
- **Investing activities**: purchase/sale of PPE, purchase/sale of investments, purchase of subsidiaries, interest received, dividends received
- **Financing activities**: proceeds from share issues, new borrowings, repayment of borrowings, lease principal payments (IFRS 16 / FRS 102 S20), dividends paid

### Exemptions
- Small companies applying Section 1A are exempt from preparing a cash flow statement
- Qualifying entities (subsidiaries) may take the exemption under FRS 102 S1.12 if parent includes them in publicly available consolidated accounts

## 6. Small Company Accounts -- Section 1A

### Qualifying Conditions (CA 2006 s382-s383)
Must satisfy at least two of three in the current and preceding year:
- Turnover not more than GBP 10.2 million
- Balance sheet total not more than GBP 5.1 million
- Average number of employees not more than 50

### Section 1A Entitlements
- Abridged balance sheet: combine line items per CA 2006 s396A options
- No requirement for a cash flow statement
- Reduced notes: only those mandated by CA 2006 Schedule 1 for small companies (accounting policies, related party transactions with directors per s413, financial commitments/guarantees/contingencies, average employees, directors' advances/credits/guarantees)
- May file abridged accounts at Companies House (balance sheet and limited notes only; no P&L or directors' report required to be filed, though they must be prepared)

## 7. Micro-Entity Accounts (FRS 105)

### Qualifying Conditions (CA 2006 s384A-s384B)
Must satisfy at least two of three:
- Turnover not more than GBP 632,000
- Balance sheet total not more than GBP 316,000
- Average number of employees not more than 10

### FRS 105 Simplifications
- Prepare only a profit and loss account and a balance sheet (no SOCI, no SOCIE, no cash flow, no notes)
- Exception: must disclose guarantees, financial commitments, and advances/credits to directors at the foot of the balance sheet
- No fair value accounting; all financial instruments at cost or amortised cost
- No deferred tax recognition
- No revaluation of PPE or investment property
- Development costs must be expensed (cannot capitalise)

## 8. Companies Act 2006 Requirements

### Formats
- Prescribed by SI 2008/410 (The Large and Medium-sized Companies and Groups (Accounts and Reports) Regulations 2008) as amended
- Cannot mix formats: once Format 1 or 2 is chosen, apply consistently
- Adaptation: line items may be combined if immaterial or if combination aids clarity (with disclosure in notes)

### True and Fair View
- CA 2006 s393: directors must not approve accounts unless satisfied they give a true and fair view
- If compliance with a standard would not give T&F, depart from the standard and disclose the departure, its reason, and its effect (CA 2006 s396(5))

### Going Concern
- Directors must assess whether going concern basis is appropriate (FRS 102 S3.8-S3.9)
- If material uncertainty exists: disclose in notes
- If going concern basis not appropriate: prepare accounts on a break-up basis

---

## 9. Filing and Approval

### Deadlines
- Private company: 9 months from the end of the accounting reference period
- Public company: 6 months from the end of the accounting reference period
- Late filing penalties: escalating from GBP 150 (private, up to 1 month late) to GBP 7,500 (public, over 6 months late)

### Board Approval
- Financial statements must be approved by the board of directors (CA 2006 s414(1))
- A director must sign the balance sheet on behalf of the board (CA 2006 s414(2))
- Date of approval is the date on the balance sheet -- post-BS events review runs until this date

### Auditor's Report
- Attached to accounts filed at Companies House (unless audit exempt)
- Audit exemption: small companies unless they are part of a group that is not small, or members holding at least 10% of shares require an audit

---

## 10. FS-Specific Statutory Accounts

**FCA-Regulated Entities**: additional disclosures required by the FCA Handbook (SUP 4, IPRU, MIFIDPRU); regulatory capital disclosures; client money disclosures if holding client assets.

**Insurance Companies (FRS 103)**: FRS 103 supplements FRS 102 for insurance contracts; specific formats for long-term business (Form 40-48) and general business (Form 11-36); technical provisions (claims outstanding, UPR, unexpired risk reserve, IBNR) presented per regulatory formats.

**Charities (SORP FRS 102)**: Statement of Financial Activities (SOFA) replaces the income statement; balance sheet shows restricted vs unrestricted funds; additional fund notes; trustees' annual report required.
